Weather Summary
Kari Kiefer
Weather Underground Forecast for Wednesday, September 05, 2012.

Hot weather will continue across the Mediterranean Sea, while cool and windy conditions spread through northern Europe on Wednesday. A strong low pressure system will continue moving eastward into Scandinavia from the Norwegian Sea. This system will push moisture onshore as well as cold air from the north. Expect heavy rain and strong winds to develop across northern Norway and northern Sweden. This will create chilly and brisk conditions across the region throughout the day. High wind advisories have been issued for most of northern Norway and Sweden. Oslo and Stockholm will remain partly cloudy and breezy with highs in the lower 60s(F)/ mid-teens(C). The system will create a cold front that extends southward from Finland, over the Baltic Sea, and into central Europe. The system will bring cooler air into the European Mainland, but is not expected to produce significant rain showers. Expect cloudy skies and breezy conditions along this front as it moves through Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania, and Poland. High pressure building in behind this system will create dry conditions and create warming trend for the British Isles and France. London will be sunny with highs in the lower 80s(F)/ upper 20s(C), while Paris will be partly cloudy with highs in the mid-70s(F)/ lower 20s(C).

To the south, a low pressure system continues spinning in the central Mediterranean Sea. Counter-clockwise flow around this system will push moisture onshore, supporting more showers and thunderstorms across Italy and the countries surrounding the Adriatic Sea. Rome will be partly cloudy with scattered showers and thunderstorms with highs in the lower 80s(F)/ upper 20s(C). Ahead of this activity, high pressure maintains hot and sunny conditions across southeastern Europe.
